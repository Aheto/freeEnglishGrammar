Detailed Lesson Plans

A comprehensive 5-day breakdown covering all grammar concepts from the provided materials, including exercises and assessments.

 Day 1 — Parts of Speech & Verb Functions

Time Topic Duration Learning Objectives Teacher Activities Resources Assessment
1 Functions of Words 40 min
  • Understand nouns vs. verbs
  • Recognize dual-function words
  • Explain how words perform different functions
  • Show examples: "I have lost my comb" (noun) vs. "I comb my hair" (verb)
  • Demonstrate: "Water is necessity" vs. "Do you water plants?"
 Word cards, example sentences, whiteboard
  • Identify word functions in given sentences
  • Create pairs of sentences showing noun/verb usage
2 Verb Forms & Tenses 40 min
  • Understand verb conjugation
  • Distinguish Simple Present vs. Past
  • Explain how verb forms change with subject and time
  • Compare: "He always walks" (Present) vs. "He walked yesterday" (Past)
  • Introduce concept of tenses indicating when actions occur
 Verb charts, timeline visuals
  • Identify tense in sample sentences
  • Convert present to past tense verbs
3 Infinitive Forms 40 min
  • Recognize infinitive with "to"
  • Understand bare infinitive
  • Explain infinitive form begins with "to" (e.g., "to walk")
  • Define bare infinitive (without "to")
  • Show dictionary listings use bare infinitive
 Dictionaries, verb lists
  • Identify infinitive forms in text
  • Convert between infinitive and bare infinitive

 Day 2 — Four Uses of Simple Present Tense

Time Topic Duration Learning Objectives Teacher Activities Resources Assessment
1 Regular Intervals 35 min
  • Use Simple Present for habitual actions
  • Explain actions at regular intervals
  • Examples: "We visit friends every Sunday", "Geese fly south every fall"
  • Practice with time expressions (every, once a week, etc.)
 Calendar visuals, habit charts
  • Write sentences about personal routines
  • Identify time expressions in sentences
2 General Truths 35 min
  • State facts using Simple Present
  • Explain use for general truths/facts
  • Examples: "Water expands when heated", "Canada lies north of US"
  • Practice with scientific and geographical facts
 Fact cards, encyclopedia excerpts
  • Write three general truth statements
  • Correct tense usage in fact statements
3 Printed Material & Special Uses 40 min
  • Use Simple Present for books/films
  • Express future/past in special cases
  • Show use in referring to printed material: "The report presents..."
  • Demonstrate describing films: "At the end, the hero finds..."
  • Explain future use: "Our plane leaves at eight tomorrow"
  • Show newspaper headline style for past events
 Book excerpts, film reviews, newspaper headlines
  • Describe a book/film plot using Simple Present
  • Write future schedule using Simple Present

 Day 3 — Verb "To Be" Complete Conjugation

Time Topic Duration Learning Objectives Teacher Activities Resources Assessment
1 Conjugation & Full Forms 40 min
  • Memorize all forms of "to be"
  • Use contractions correctly
  • Present conjugation chart: I am, You are, He/She/It is, We are, They are
  • Teach contractions: I'm, You're, He's, She's, It's, We're, They're
  • Note: Modern English uses same forms for "it" (singular/plural)
 Conjugation charts, pronoun cards
  • Recite conjugation from memory
  • Convert full forms to contractions
2 Affirmative Statements & Questions 40 min
  • Form correct affirmative statements
  • Create questions with word order reversal
  • Explain verb follows subject in statements: "I am awake", "They are ready"
  • Show questions reverse order: "Am I awake?", "Are they ready?"
  • Emphasize capital letters and punctuation (period vs. question mark)
 Example sentences, punctuation cards
  • Convert statements to questions (Exercise 2)
  • Correct punctuation usage
3 Negative Forms 40 min
  • Form negative statements
  • Create negative questions
  • Add "not" after verb for negatives: "I am not awake"
  • Negative questions: reverse order + "not" after subject
  • Examples: "Am I not awake?", "Are they not ready?"
 Negative sentence strips
  • Convert affirmatives to negatives (Exercise 3)
  • Form negative questions (Exercise 4)

 Day 4 — Contractions & Tag Questions

Time Topic Duration Learning Objectives Teacher Activities Resources Assessment
1 Contractions in Spoken English 40 min
  • Use contractions in negative questions
  • Understand "isn't", "aren't" formation
  • Show contraction placement in negative questions
  • Examples: "Aren't you awake?", "Isn't he awake?"
  • Practice: "Are you not" → "Aren't you"
 Contraction charts, audio examples
  • Convert formal to contracted forms
  • Practice spoken contractions
2 Special Rule: "I am not" 35 min
  • Understand "aren't I" usage
  • Distinguish formal vs. informal
  • Explain no universal contraction for "I am not"
  • Informal: "amn't I" → "aren't I" (e.g., "Aren't I awake?")
  • Formal: No contraction acceptable; use "am I not"
  • Clarify "aren't I" not grammatically correct in formal writing
 Formal/informal comparison charts
  • Identify appropriate usage contexts
  • Convert between formal and informal
3 Tag Questions 45 min
  • Form tag questions correctly
  • Repeat subjects and verbs
  • Define tag questions: added at sentence end, meaning "isn't it so?"
  • Show pattern: repeat subject and verb from statement
  • Examples: "I am awake, am I not?", "You are awake, aren't you?"
  • Note: Use contractions except with "I" subject
 Tag question examples, Exercise 5
  • Add tag questions to statements (Exercise 5)
  • Correct contraction usage in tags

 All exercises from pages 30-32

 Assessment worksheets

Differentiation Strategies

Tailored support to ensure every learner masters English grammar concepts — whether they need extra help or an extra challenge.

For Struggling Learners

  • Provide conjugation charts and verb tables as reference tools during exercises
  • Use color-coding to distinguish subjects, verbs, and objects in sentence formation
  • Offer sentence frames and templates for forming questions and negatives
  • Practice contractions through repetitive oral drills and audio examples
  • Break down tag questions into two steps: identify subject/verb, then add negative tag

For Advanced Learners

  • Challenge them to write paragraphs using all four uses of Simple Present tense
  • Introduce irregular verb patterns beyond "to be" for early exposure
  • Task them with creating dialogues demonstrating formal vs. informal contractions
  • Encourage analysis of newspaper headlines using Simple Present for past events
  • Have them teach tag question formation to peers as reinforcement
